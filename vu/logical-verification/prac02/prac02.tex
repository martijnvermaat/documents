The following derivation of $(A \rightarrow B) \rightarrow (B \rightarrow C) \rightarrow A \rightarrow C$
using natural deduction shows that this formula is a tautology of propositional logic.

\begin{prooftree}
\AxiomC{$[(B \rightarrow C)^{y}]$}
  \AxiomC{$[(A \rightarrow B)^{x}]$}
  \AxiomC{$[A^{z}]$}
  \RightLabel{$E\rightarrow$}
\BinaryInfC{$B$}
\RightLabel{$E\rightarrow$}
\BinaryInfC{$C$}
\RightLabel{$I[z]\rightarrow$}
\UnaryInfC{$A \rightarrow C$}
\RightLabel{$I[y]\rightarrow$}
\UnaryInfC{$(B \rightarrow C) \rightarrow A \rightarrow C$}
\RightLabel{$I[x]\rightarrow$}
\UnaryInfC{$(A \rightarrow B) \rightarrow (B \rightarrow C) \rightarrow A \rightarrow C$}
\end{prooftree}

In the type derivation below, we use $\Gamma$ for the set $x:A \rightarrow B, y:B \rightarrow C, z:A$.

\begin{prooftree}
\AxiomC{$\Gamma \vdash y \: : \: B \rightarrow C$}
  \AxiomC{$\Gamma \vdash x \: : \: A \rightarrow B$}
  \AxiomC{$\Gamma \vdash z \: : \: A$}
  \BinaryInfC{$\Gamma \vdash x \, z \: : \: B$}
\BinaryInfC{$\Gamma \vdash y (x \, z) \: : \: C$}
\UnaryInfC{$x:A \rightarrow B, y:B \rightarrow C \vdash \lambda z:A \, . \, y (x \, z) \: : \:
  A \rightarrow C$}
\UnaryInfC{$x:A \rightarrow B \vdash \lambda (y:B \rightarrow C) (z:A) \, . \, y (x \, z) \: : \:
  (B \rightarrow C) \rightarrow A \rightarrow C$}
\UnaryInfC{$\vdash \lambda (x:A \rightarrow B) (y:B \rightarrow C) (z:A) \, . \, y (x \, z) \: : \:
  (A \rightarrow B) \rightarrow (B \rightarrow C) \rightarrow A \rightarrow C$}
\end{prooftree}
